Choose an end-point assessment organisation

Once the apprenticeship training is completed, an independent assessment will take place, this is called an 'end-point assessment'. The assessment is part of the process as a guarantee that the apprenticeship training is of high quality.

It also gives the apprentice the opportunity to demonstrate what they've learnt throughout the apprenticeship. (This assessment only applies for apprenticeship standards and not for frameworks).

This will include an assessment of the apprentice's:

  • knowledge
  • skills
  • behaviours

Your apprentice can't achieve an apprenticeship standard without satisfying all the requirements of the assessment plan, including the end-point assessment.

When to choose an end-point assessment organisation (EPAO)

It's a good idea to choose an EPAO early in the process, ideally at the beginning of the process when you choose your training provider. It's a good idea because of things.

Something about being able to choose later if you want.

End-point assessment cost

We expect that the cost of the end-point assessment should not usually exceed 20% of the funding band maximum.

This cost is seperate to the overall cost agreed with the training provider for the apprenticeship and you should negotiate this with the EPAO.

Although the training provider will be involved in arrangements for the end-point assessment, the assessment itself must be independent.

You must contract with the end-point assessment organisation and have a written agreement in place to make payment to them for conducting the end-point assessment.

Where to find an end-point assessment organisation

You must select an organisation to deliver the end-point assessment from the register of end-point assessment organisations and agree to a price with this organisation for the end-point assessment.

Qualification

When your apprentice successfully completes their apprenticeship they will be awarded a certificate.

The end-point assessment organisation will request the certificate on your behalf.
