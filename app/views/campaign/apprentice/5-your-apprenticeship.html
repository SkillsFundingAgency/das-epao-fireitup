What to bring, and other useful info

Your employer will be in touch, to let you know your working hours, and when they’d like you to start on your first day. Ideally they will also send you a list of what to bring along.

You will probably be sent a list covering the following:

  • what you will need to bring - notebook/pens/ID
  • what time you need to arrive
  • how your should dress - suit or jeans
  • how much money you will need to bring for lunch etc
  • who you should ask for when you arrive
  • where to get the bus/train or park your car

Meet your new team

When you start your apprenticeship, you’ll probably be introduced to your line manager.

They will show you around, answer any questions and invite you to regular catch-ups to see how you’re getting along. They’ll also keep in touch with your training provider – wherever you’re doing your studying – to make sure your whole apprenticeship is going well.

First an apprenticeship, now a career!

Once your apprenticeship is up and running, it’s always worth discussing your future career with your current employer, as well as doing some research yourself.

Could you progress from apprentice to permanent staff? Or take your new skills somewhere new? Perhaps another apprenticeship in a totally different company?

The sooner you start to take control of the new trail you’re blazing, the better the ride will be.
